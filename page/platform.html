Financial Aid Transparency

Plain and simple. The financial aid process should not cause dread. Student Government must actively engage the administration in a conversation about cost of attendance and advocate for counseling resources so students can navigate and understand all of the options available to fund their degrees.



Freshman 15

Fifteen initiatives to involve freshman in Student Government like never before. Beginning on Week One, Student Government should help freshman acclimate with information sessions that cover housing on and off campus, scholarships, financial aid, dropping classes, and locating academic and safety resources. Student Government should also foster a collaborative environment with increased funding for freshman tutoring services and greater connectivity in the FIG programs.



Forty Acres OS 2.0

Leverage technology to enrich education, improve student life, and maximize safety. This begins with conversations with administrators on campus that introduce student concerns. Student Government should advocate for improvements in UTPD's online presence, faster safety email and text warnings for possible dangers on and off campus, and student-frinedly registration, housing, and financial aid websites.
